L1: THE TWO ROLES

People blur these roles and lose control. Let's fix that.

ROLE WHO OWNS WHY IT MATTERS
AUTHOR / DIRECTOR YOU Vision ¬∑ Taste ¬∑ Emotion ¬∑ Intent ¬∑ Decisions Your unique voice lives here
MATERIAL & ALLY AI + TOOLS Speed ¬∑ Variations ¬∑ Execution ¬∑ Raw ideas Great for efficiency, no soul without your guidance
DAILY REMINDER

Say it before every session: "I am the author. The AI is talented material."

HOW IT WORKS

You (Author): Set emotional core: "Make it feel like finding an old letter from someone you've lost"

AI (Material): Provides 4 visual options

You (Director): Pick the one that hits hardest, refine with negatives: "never sentimental, zero closure energy"

WHERE PEOPLE FAIL

  • Giving AI too much power: "Just make something cool" = you're not directing
  • Over-controlling technical details: Describing every pixel = missing the emotional point
  • Accepting first results: No iteration = AI's voice, not yours

The balance: You lead with emotion and intent. AI executes with speed and variation. You select, refine, push.

GOLDEN RULE

Spend 80% of time in Level 1 & 2. Level 3 is for finishing ONLY.

LEVEL 1: VIBE/ATTITUDE (Discovery)

Example: "Quiet regret at dusk, melancholic warmth, never hopeful"

When: Starting projects, finding mood, exploring ideas

Why it works: Short = few tokens = less AI drift = your voice dominates

LEVEL 2: ROLE + GOAL (Daily Work)

Example: "You're my talented first AD. Create 4 variations of a crumbling apartment at golden hour. Rate each 1-10 for melancholic warmth. Never cute, never hopeful. Wait for my choice."

When: Iterative work, refinement, collaboration

Why it works: Clear role, clear goal, built-in guard rails

LEVEL 3: ULTRA-PRECISE (Finishing)

Example: Multi-paragraph technical specifications

When: Final polish, client-ready output

Warning: More words = more tokens = more AI interpretation = drift risk

WHY TOKENS MATTER

Tokens are chunks AI reads (usually 3-5 chars). Long prompts = more tokens = slower, more expensive, higher drift.

Short vibe prompts = fewer tokens = faster, cheaper, YOUR intent dominates.

L3: FOUR PROMPT PATTERNS

Memorize these templates‚Äîthey carry 80% of the weight.

1. VIBE + CONTRADICTION

Forces AI out of defaults, creates tension.

Chaotic yet surgically precise, dream-like but brutally honest, 
like the morning after a fight you know you lost

2. DIRECTOR'S INSTRUCTION

Keeps you in charge, turns AI into collaborator.

You are my extremely talented but slightly over-eager first AD. 
Suggest three strong directions, but do not decide‚Äîpresent 
options clearly and wait for my choice.

3. SELF-REFLECTION ANCHOR

Protects your voice by forcing AI to mirror your taste.

Before you generate, first describe in 2 sentences how my 
personal taste would probably feel about a crumbling brutalist 
interior at golden hour. Only after that, create the image.

4. ITERATIVE POWER LOOP

Best repeating workflow for maintaining control.

Show me four variations. Rate each one yourself from my point 
of view (1-10, melancholic warmth, never cute). Then wait for 
which direction I want to push further.
PRO TIP

Mix patterns. Start with Vibe+Contradiction, iterate with Power Loop, finish with Self-Reflection check.

L4: TWO CONTROL LEVERS

Most people pile on positives. That's weak. These levers flip the script.

LEVER 1: NEGATIVE SPACE INSTRUCTIONS

Forbidden zones that force originality.

never cute
zero corporate/marketing energy
absolutely no inspirational quote energy
do not try to be beautiful
no lens flare, no dramatic sky, no golden ratio bullshit
avoid symmetry at all costs

WHY NEGATIVES WORK

AI is trained on billions of images. Its defaults = what appears most often = generic corporate stock photos.

Negatives carve out space by blocking the defaults, forcing AI to search deeper into its training.

LEVER 2: EMOTIONAL POV ANCHORING

Stop describing surfaces. Describe the gut punch.

Make it feel like the moment you realize you were wrong about 
someone for three years

Make it feel like the last cigarette before you quit forever

Like finding an old mixtape from an ex you haven't thought 
about in years

The quiet resentment of scrolling past someone's perfect life 
at 3 AM

WHY EMOTIONAL ANCHORS WORK

They tap into universal human experience. AI can't "feel" but it's been trained on millions of texts describing feelings.

The anchor connects abstract emotion to concrete visual/sonic patterns.

THE FORMULA

Negatives carve out space. Anchors fill it with your truth. Together = your voice.

CORE SHIFT

You are Producer/Director. AI is your band or animation crew. Hold the leash tight.

PART 1: MUSIC COMPOSITION

Defaults lean toward upbeat, major-key polish. Here's how to break that.

Level 1: Vibe + Sonic Texture

Negatives Are Essential

Emotional Anchors for Music

dissonant chords, no catharsis

PART 2: ANIMATION DIRECTION

Defaults are Disney-smooth and "cute." You want awkward, lived-in motion.

Level 1: Vibe + Motion Intent

Quiet regret scene ‚Äî camera hesitates then drifts slowly, 
objects barely shift, never fluid or cinematic

Negatives Dominate

no exaggerated motion or bouncy physics
zero cute expressions
avoid perfect timing ‚Äî add awkward holds
never smooth easing
no dramatic camera moves

Emotional Anchors for Motion

Motion feels like dragging through a memory you can't escape ‚Äî 
slow, reluctant, no payoff

Like trying to leave a room but your feet won't move

The heavy weight of knowing it's over but not wanting to admit it

COMBINED WORKFLOW

  1. Start with still image (Vibe + Contradiction)
  2. Direct motion (Add motion negatives + anchor)
  3. Layer music (Separate prompt, same emotional core)
  4. Add ambient sound (Subtle, never overpowering)
  5. Composite (CapCut/DaVinci, minimal editing)
